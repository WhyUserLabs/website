Manifesto

Marketing is the only discipline that tests in production.

Engineering gets a staging environment. Finance gets an audit. Marketing gets opening night, real budget, real buyers, no rehearsal. We built the stress test it never had.

01

Testing in production is malpractice.

Engineering has staging. Finance has audits. Sales runs role plays. Marketing ships straight to opening night, real budget, real buyers, no rehearsal. Every other discipline would call that reckless.

02

B2B is decided by a committee, not a click.

A champion loves it. A CFO vetoes it. A security lead raises one doubt and the deal stalls. The veto is silent. It never reaches your CRM. No analytics tool will tell you which seat killed the deal.

03

Opinion does not move budget. Evidence does.

“I think the hero is weak” loses every meeting. “The economic buyer bounced at the ROI section, here is the proof” wins it. The job is not a better opinion. It is the receipt.

04

AI made it worse, not better.

Infinite throughput, zero grounding. More copy, faster, none of it tested. A chatbot hands you one confident opinion. One opinion is not a test. Loop it thirty times and you get one opinion thirty ways.

05

A real test has four properties.

Grounded in your actual buyers, not invented personas. Adversarial, hundreds of agents across designed states. Deterministic, so a fix is a fix, not model drift. And graded, so every call is marked HIT or MISS against what really happened.

06

Do not trust us. Grade us.

We do not ask for faith. Run WhyUser on a campaign you already know the ending to. If it catches what actually happened, you have your answer, from your data, not our claims.

The bet

See the silent veto before a real buyer does. Fail in the test, win in the market.
